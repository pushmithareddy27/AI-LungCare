Understanding the disease

What COPD is — and why timing changes everything

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease is a progressive narrowing of the airways. Lung function lost to COPD cannot be regained — which is why early detection is the single highest-leverage intervention available.

🫁

What is COPD?

A group of progressive lung diseases, including chronic bronchitis and emphysema, that block airflow and make breathing difficult over time.

⏱

Why early diagnosis matters

COPD is usually silent until 50–60% of lung function is already lost. Early risk scoring lets treatment begin while intervention still has the most impact.

📉

Cost of late detection

Late-stage COPD drives repeated hospital admissions. Continuous monitoring catches exacerbations days before they become emergencies.
